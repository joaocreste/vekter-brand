01 — The concept

A name that points somewhere.

Most consultancies are named after a person, a place, or a promise. Vekter is named after a direction. It takes Victor — the founder's second given name — and rewrites it through the logic of physics: a vector is a quantity with magnitude and direction. Not a dot. Not a destination. A push.

The identity is built around a single idea: every engagement should leave the client measurably further along than where it found them. The arrow isn't decoration. It's the thesis.

02 — The mark

One arrow. Four surfaces.

The logo is a lowercase wordmark drawn with the warmth of a pen, anchored by a geometric support line set in capital letters. The t breaks its own crossbar into a right-facing arrow — this is the fixed, non-negotiable element. Everything else is variable.

The t is the pivot.

In the handwritten original, the crossbar of the t overshoots the letter and becomes the arrow. Every digital execution must preserve this — the arrow never lives above or below the wordmark; it is the wordmark's top-most stroke.

Three voices, no noise.

05 — Voice

How Vekter sounds on the page.

I.

Specific, not generic.

Never "stakeholders." Always "the operating partner at Aktia." Never "synergies." Always "a 14% reduction in CAPEX by month nine."

II.

Directional, not declarative.

Sentences should move. Prefer verbs of trajectory (shift, compound, unlock, compress) over verbs of presence (are, have, show).

III.

Literate, not corporate.

Write like a memo from someone who has also read a novel. Short sentences. Long thinking. No jargon that a smart operator wouldn't use with her own hands.

The system, applied.
